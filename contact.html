FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common questions about Erasmus+ programs and applications.

To apply for an Erasmus+ exchange program, you need to: 1) Check your eligibility with your home university, 2) Find partner universities through our agreements list, 3) Complete the online application form, 4) Submit required documents including academic transcripts and language certificates, 5) Wait for the selection results. Applications typically open twice a year for fall and spring semesters.
Erasmus+ provides grants to cover travel and subsistence costs. Grant amounts vary depending on the destination country (grouped into three categories). Students receive monthly grants between €300-€450 for studies and €450-€600 for traineeships. Additional top-ups are available for students with fewer opportunities or those using green travel options.
Language requirements vary by host university and program. Most programs require at least B1-B2 level in the instruction language (typically English). You can demonstrate proficiency through TOEFL, IELTS, or institutional certificates. Erasmus+ also provides free Online Linguistic Support (OLS) for language preparation before and during your exchange.
For study exchanges, the mobility period ranges from 3 to 12 months per study cycle (Bachelor's, Master's, Doctorate). Traineeships can last from 2 to 12 months. You can participate multiple times as long as the total doesn't exceed 12 months per study cycle. Short-term blended programs (BIPs) are also available, combining virtual and physical mobility.
Yes! Academic recognition is a fundamental principle of Erasmus+. Before your exchange, you'll sign a Learning Agreement approved by both universities. This document guarantees that credits earned abroad will be fully recognized. The ECTS (European Credit Transfer System) ensures transparent credit conversion across European institutions.
